Principal Sends a Letter


Dear Students and Parents of Thomas Jefferson High School,

I am writing this message for all of the TJ community to address a severe problem we face within our school. As a school for science and technology, Thomas Jefferson High School must maintain its high academic standards along with its moral integrity.

I would like to congratulate and thank all the students who actively contribute to this community by spending hours distracted by facebook, surfing reddit, and watching hundreds of random youtube videos. Because of our collective commitment to procrastination, TJHSST has been named the top high school in the country according to Newsweek Magazine. Some of the greatest moments in human history have been the result of countless hours spent wasting time online. Take for example, the Cuban Missile Crisis, which was the result of commanders on both sides being too engaged in a game of Words With Friends to notice that the Soviets had almost set up missiles in Cuba. Great moments like these are not easy to achieve-- we must continually strive for them. It is because of this culture that our school regularly sends students to colleges such as Harvard, Stanford, Princeton, Yale, and The University of Phoenix. To celebrate the achievements of these wonderfully lazy students, celebratory cake will be available in the library.

As part of the renovation, we will be devoting a small sum of $40 million to the creation of dedicated social commons areas with free gigabit wifi hotspots, rejuvenation (napping) stations, and mobile device charging ports. These rooms will be soundproof and will have no clocks to prevent outside distractions. Students should feel free to leave classes anytime to enjoy the facilitation of these rooms.

Despite all the achievements these students have made for TJ, there are a handful of students who continue to be “tryhards”, as their fellow peers put it. They always study and ace all their assessments. They score well on tests that ultimately lessens the curves and hurts the grades of other students. We must address these groups of students who are harming the school environment. I would like to encourage students to collaborate with each other and promote procrastination throughout the student body. We would like to practice the policies of same-boat, not cut-throat. These high test scores are cut-throat actions that we cannot tolerate in our school. If these actions continue, I will personally seek vengeance upon these disobedient, worthless maggots, and their cut-throat behavior will result in their cut from school, college, and from every job they plan on holding.

Sincerely,

Ph.D M.D. B.S. your friendly neighbor and your teacher and your principal and captain of integrity and hero of justice and Gooooo~ Colonials Dr. Glozer

Post Scriptum: We would like to remind all student respect the rules of not drinking or eating in the library
